How does the knight move?

Like an "L"

Most chess players already know that knights jump over other pieces and moves two squares vertically and one horizontally or the other way around but today we are going to go further than that

Let's take a look at the picture

The squares with a 1 written on them represent the squares that the knight can move to in 1 movement

That means the safest squares are the squares marked with a 4!

Now take another look at the squares labeled with a 3

The ones next to the knight are excellent for a king to counterattack the knight, and the other ones will keep your king or queen very safe

This king looks really safe now, right?
